I was trained as an archaeologist and a signal processing engineer, and I now work in digital psychiatry at Beth Israel Deaconess Medical Center and Harvard Medical School. I am currently studying what happens when generative AI models are used as mental health tools. I am broadly interested in media archaeology, the history of language technologies, and the gap between what AI companies know about their own systems and what independent researchers are allowed to measure.

Creative

My creative writing orbits the central theme of miscommunication. What happens when the systems we build to carry meaning between people start distorting that meaning, or generating meaning of their own, or failing in ways that nobody involved can see from inside the failure.
